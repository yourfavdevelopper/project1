Who is Plácido Domingo?

Plácido Domingo, born on January 21, 1941, in Madrid, Spain, stands as one of the preeminent figures in the world of classical music. Renowned for his extraordinary tenor voice, Domingo has enjoyed a prolific career as an opera singer, conductor, and arts administrator. His vocal prowess has been showcased in an extensive repertoire, encompassing a diverse range of roles from the operas of composers like Verdi, Puccini, Wagner, and others. With a portfolio boasting over 150 different roles, Domingo's artistry has left an indelible mark on the classical music landscape. Beyond his accomplishments as a singer, Plácido Domingo has made significant contributions as a conductor and administrator in the world of opera. He has taken on leadership roles in major opera companies and has conducted numerous orchestras. His commitment to the promotion of opera and classical music has made him a revered figure on the global stage
