Who is Ibrahim Tatlises?

İbrahim Tatlıses, born İbrahim Tatlı on January 1, 1952, in Şanlıurfa, Turkey, is a highly revered figure in Turkish folk music. Renowned for his powerful and distinct voice, he has left an indelible mark on the cultural landscape of Turkey. Adopting the stage name Tatlıses, meaning "sweet-voiced" in Turkish, he rose to prominence in the 1970s and 1980s, becoming one of the most influential Turkish folk singers. His repertoire includes a multitude of hits, such as "Mavi Mavi," "Gel Gör Beni Aşk Neyledi," and "Beyaz Gül Kızıl Crown," among many others. İbrahim Tatlıses's performances are characterized by the emotive depth of Turkish folk and arabesque music, showcasing his mastery of these traditional genres. Beyond his musical achievements, Tatlıses has also made significant contributions to the Turkish film and television industry, taking on acting roles in addition to his singing career. However, his life and career have not been without challenges and controversies. İbrahim Tatlıses remains an iconic and influential figure in Turkish music and culture.
